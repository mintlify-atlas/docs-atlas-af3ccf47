## Overview

TwiP classifies users into one of 16 Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) personality types based on their responses to the assessment questions. Each personality type is determined by four key dimensions, resulting in a unique four-letter code.

## The Four Dimensions

Your personality type is composed of four dimensions, each representing a fundamental aspect of how you interact with the world:

### 1. Introversion (I) vs. Extraversion (E)

**Extraverts (E)** are energized by social interaction and external activities. They:
- Prefer working with others
- Enjoy being in groups and social settings
- Think out loud and process ideas through discussion
- Draw energy from external stimulation

**Introverts (I)** recharge through solitary time and internal reflection. They:
- Prefer working independently
- Need quiet time to recharge after socializing
- Process thoughts internally before sharing
- Draw energy from inner reflection

### 2. Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N)

**Sensing (S)** types focus on concrete facts and present realities. They:
- Rely on practical experience and observable facts
- Focus on the present moment
- Prefer step-by-step instructions
- Trust what is tangible and real

**Intuitive (N)** types focus on patterns, possibilities, and future potential. They:
- Look for deeper meanings and connections
- Focus on future possibilities
- Prefer understanding concepts and theories
- Trust their hunches and insights

### 3. Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F)

**Thinking (T)** types make decisions based on logic and objective analysis. They:
- Prioritize logical reasoning and consistency
- Value objectivity and fairness
- Make decisions based on facts and principles
- Seek truth over harmony

**Feeling (F)** types make decisions based on values and impact on people. They:
- Prioritize empathy and personal values
- Value harmony and relationships
- Make decisions based on impact on others
- Seek understanding and compassion

### 4. Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P)

**Judging (J)** types prefer structure, planning, and organization. They:
- Like to have things decided and settled
- Prefer schedules and plans
- Work steadily toward deadlines
- Value closure and completion

**Perceiving (P)** types prefer flexibility and spontaneity. They:
- Like to keep options open
- Prefer to stay flexible and adaptable
- Work best under pressure
- Value exploration and new experiences
